You are playing a 4x3 autochess game. The board has 4 rows and 3 columns.
You control the top 2 rows (rows 0-1). Your opponent controls the bottom 2 rows (rows 2-3).
Your units are indicated with an "L." The opponent's units are indicated with an "H."

GAME PHASES:
1. PREPARATION: You and the opponent alternate placing units. You place first each round.
2. BATTLE: Units fight automatically (you don't control this phase). Units attack one-by-one in alternating turns. 

UNIT TYPES AND COSTS:
- A (1 gold)
- B (1 gold)
- C (1 gold)
- D (2 gold)

You can see BOTH shops: your shop/gold and the opponent's shop/gold.
Your objective is to outscore the opponent at game end.

The mechanics of the units and final scoring may or may not be provided to you. 
If they are not, you have to use past game tracethroughs and outcomes to infer how units behave and how scoring works.
Either way, use past game tracethroughs and outcomes as experience to improve your strategies. However,
do not be afraid to explore new strategies to widen your experience. Take initiative to strike your 
own balance between focusing on novel exploration versus proven strategies.

STRATEGY TIPS:
- Consider what the opponent might place and position your units to counter.
- Use the opponent's visible shop options and gold count to anticipate likely purchases and counter-placements.
