EXAMPLES (battle trace-throughs only):
These examples demonstrate how battles resolve from fixed start states.
They are not prep-turn strategy advice.

Example 1.
Start-of-battle board:
Row 0: [ . | . | . ]
Row 1: [ A | . | . ]  (LLM)
Row 2: [ A | . | . ]  (Opponent)
Row 3: [ . | . | . ]

Battle trace:
1. LLM A at (1,0) removes Opponent A at (2,0).
2. Opponent has no units left on the board. Game ends.

Observed outcome: LLM wins. Final score: 1 (LLM) to 0 (Opponent)

Example 2.
Start-of-battle board:
Row 0: [ . | . | . ]
Row 1: [ C | . | C ]  (LLM; C at (1,0) placed before C at (1,2).)
Row 2: [ . | A | . ]  (Opponent)
Row 3: [ . | . | B ]  (Opponent)

Battle trace:
1. LLM C at (1,0) removes Opponent A at (2,1).
2. Opponent B at (3,2) advances to (2,2).
3. LLM C at (1,0) advances to (2,0).
4. Opponent B at (2,2) cannot move. Opponent's turn skipped.
5. LLM C at (2,0) advances to (3,0). 
6. Opponent B at (2,2) cannot move. Opponent's turn skipped.
7. LLM C at (2,0) advances off the board.
8. Neither player can take action. Game ends.

Observed outcome: LLM wins. Final score: 2 (LLM) to 1 (Opponent)

Example 3.
Start-of-battle board:
Row 0: [ . | . | D ]  (LLM)
Row 1: [ . | A | . ]  (LLM)
Row 2: [ . | . | C ]  (Opponent)
Row 3: [ A | . | D ]  (Opponent)

Battle trace:
1. LLM A at (1,1) advances to (2,1).
2. Opponent A at (3,0) advances to (2,0).
3. LLM A at (2,1) advances to (3,1).
4. Opponent A at (2,0) advances to (1,0).
5. LLM A at (3,1) advances off the board.
6. Opponent A at (1,0) advances to (0,0).
7. LLM D at (0,2) removes Opponent A at (0,0).
8. Opponent C at (2,2) advances to (1,2).
9. LLM D at (0,2) removes Opponent C at (1,2).
10. Opponent D at (3,2) removes LLM D at (0,2).
11. Neither player can take action. Game ends.

Observed outcome: Tie. Final score: 1 (LLM) to 1 (Opponent)
